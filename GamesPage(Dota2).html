  • Heroes

    • Players take control of "heroes," which are fictional characters with special skills and responsibilities including initiator, support, and carry. As the game goes on, heroes can level up their power by earning gold and experience points (XP).

  • Map


    • On a symmetrical map with three lanes—top, middle (mid), and bottom—the game is played. The defensive towers that defend each route that leads to the opposing base

  • Creep Waves


    • Waves of computer-controlled creatures known as creeps periodically appear in each lane and advance toward the opponent base. These creeps can be farmed by players for experience and gold.

  • Roshan


    • A formidable neutral boss, Roshan is situated in the pit close to the river. The Aegis of the Immortal is a potent weapon that gives heroes a second chance at life, and teams must destroy Roshan to obtain it.





Dota 2 is a multiplayer online battle arena (MOBA) game developed by Valve. It features two teams of five players, each controlling a unique hero, and the objective is to destroy the opposing team's ancient. Dota 2 is known for its deep strategic gameplay and a vast roster of heroes.Players earn gold and experience by defeating creeps and heroes, using them to buy items and level up. Success relies on teamwork, strategic map control, effective itemization, and adapting to the evolving game dynamics. Coordinated pushes, map awareness, and hero selection are key elements in achieving victory in this complex and highly strategic game.










  • Jungle


    • The map has neutral encampment amid the jungle. Heroes that specialize in jungling can gain experience and gold by defeating these camps.

  • Roles


    • Usually, each player on the team has a designated role, such as midlaner, carry, support, or offlaner. Strategy and teamwork are essential for success.

  • Matches and Ranking


    • There are ranked and casual match types in Dota 2. Players with comparable skill levels are paired in ranked matches using a matchmaking system.
